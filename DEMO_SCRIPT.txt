[1:00 - ARCHITECTURE]
(show assets/architecture_diagram.png)
Let us look at the architecture. When a property risk questionnaire is submitted, it enters our Security Checkpoint node. This node scrubs policyholder PII, flags prompt injections, and applies domain restrictions. (pause)
Next, the scrubbed questionnaire is sent to our Lead Underwriter Orchestrator. (pause)
The orchestrator delegates to two specialized LlmAgents: the Compliance Validator Agent and the Scoring Agent. (pause)
These agents make use of our local MCP server tools to pull live fire protection standards, underwriting guidelines, and risk scoring matrices. (pause)
The findings converge on a Decision Gate. If the calculated score is in the borderline zone of 40 to 75 points, the workflow pauses at the HITL Review Node, requiring manual sign-off from a licensed underwriter. Otherwise, it routes directly to Finalize Decision to output a structured JSON verdict.
